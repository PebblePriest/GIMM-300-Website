My Work

Check out my past animations as well as some other fun projects!

Animations Done at CWI

These animations were created during my time at CWI, when I did not know what path I was going to follow. The class I did them in was a Animation History class, so the goal was to teach how and why these styles were created over the years.

Bouncy Ball

This animation was made using the Draw and Erase technigue. This was the first real time animation I had ever done, never dabbling in it before this class.

Skeleton Doot

Using the Cut Out animation technique, we created our own animation using printers to move them between each scan. I chose this skeleton at the time since it was close to Halloween.

Reborn

Based off of the factions from the hit MMORPG Star Wars The Old Republic, this animation was a picture by picture drawing that tried to grasp the desctruction of two sides into one.

Octopus

The goal for this animation was to make a looping animation that could look like something being created and disappearing with a logical reason behind it. My choice was an octopus since it could get big then small and run away.

Animations done at BSU

All of the projects in this section were either done solo or with a group during my time with the GIMM program.

Plant Conciousness

One of the first big projects I did with a group at BSU, using our knowledge and understanding from reading Biocentrism: How Life and Consciousness Are the Keys to Understanding the True Nature of the Universe, we created a comic that helps portray a certain theme from the book. This was done with a group of 5 people over the course of a 2 to 3 weeks. Dialogue done by Ross Pataro.(Flash Needed to View)

Beeomancer Music Video

A music video using minimal knowledge of Adobe Animate as well as being the first big project of my GIMM career, this music video was a creation of one of the most interesting Dungeon and Dragons ideas's I have had. A Bee Keeper who resurrects his dead bee's as his weapons, instead of the usual undead army, it is an undead swarm.

Loss of a Friend

Not technically an animation, but a fun use of a green screen and some tweeking of said screen to make an adventure into the ruins of a lost civilization. This was done for the GIMM class covering audio and video, which we used Adobe Premiere to do both for this project.

Interactive Comic

A animation done with a group of 5, we made a mobile interactive comic that helped portray a story. In this animation, a man relives a nightmare over and over again until he is able to escape it and understand the true meaning behind what he was seeing.(Flash needed to view)
